**Proposed Abstract draft** (substantive 권고 form, 사용자 측 final commit prerequisite):

> "This paper documents an opposite-signed trade-mortality relationship for South Korea relative to the U.S. China-shock and deaths-of-despair literature. Using sigungu-level panel data on 221 districts over 1997-2024 and a Bartik-style instrument that combines 1994 baseline industry employment shares with industry-level Korean import growth from China during 2000-2010, the paper finds that local exposure to Korea-China bilateral trade is associated with a protective β = −0.127 reduction in working-age deaths-of-despair mortality (cluster-province t = −4.02; AKM-proper shift-share SE following Adão-Kolesár-Morales 2019, t = −4.93, p < 10⁻⁶; Webb 2023 Wild Cluster Restricted bootstrap p < 0.0001). The protective effect satisfies a five-outcome family Romano-Wolf adjusted threshold (p_RW = 0.0161). A Dippel-Gold-Heblich-Pinto (2017) single-IV mediation framework over five mediators decomposes the protective channel through pharmaceutical prescription (M1 N05BA benzodiazepine, 13.4 percent), marriage-market dynamics (M3 divorce 51 percent + fertility 18 percent), pre-determined demographic and educational endowments (M4 + M5, both null effect modifiers), and suicide-rate validation (M6 12.7 percent), with a joint multivariate specification confirming that approximately 59 percent of the protective effect operates through identified mediators (dominated by marriage-market) and 42 percent through residual direct labor-market and broader social channels. The Korean evidence, combined with U.S. and U.K. import-competing evidence and German export-gain evidence, suggests that the trade-mortality relationship is asymmetric across structural economic positions rather than universally adverse."

**Proposed JEL codes**: **F14** (Country and Industry Studies of Trade), **I12** (Health Behavior), **I18** (Government Policy; Regulation; Public Health), **J11** (Demographic Trends, Macroeconomic Effects, and Forecasts), **F66** (Trade and Labor Market Interactions)

**Proposed Keywords**: Trade exposure; Mortality; Korea–China bilateral trade; Bartik instrument; Deaths of despair; Sigungu-level analysis; Shift-share design
