# § 7. Mechanism

## 7.1 Mediator framework and analytic sample

The mechanism analysis in this section decomposes the protective effect of Korea-China bilateral trade exposure on working-age deaths-of-despair mortality (Section 5) through a Dippel, Gold, Heblich, and Pinto (2017) single-IV mediation framework. The Pre-Analysis Plan v4.5 (§ 9) commits to a six-mediator framework spanning pharmaceutical prescription access (M1), psychiatric diagnosis (M2; deprecated due to a discontinued endpoint in the public HIRA disease API during Phase 1 — see Section 8.3.4), family-aggregate marriage market measures (M3 KOSIS family aggregates), pre-determined cohort sex ratios (M4 z_m_marital from MDIS census 1975-1995 cohorts), university-distance baseline characteristics (M5 z_m_education from KEDI 1985 yearbook of 171 four-year universities), and KOSTAT suicide rates as an outcome-side validation channel (M6). With M2 deprecated, the active mediator set comprises M1, M3, M4, M5, and M6.

### 7.1.1 HIRA pharmaceutical mediator (M1) sample restriction

**Sigungu unit definition.** Throughout this paper, the sigungu unit is defined per sigungu_crosswalk v1, which retains the urban autonomous-district structure of 일반시 (e.g., 청주 상당구 33041 + 흥덕구 33043 are treated as separate h_codes; the 통합청주시 parent collapse to h_code 33040 is **not** applied). The intersection 147 sample includes 14 of 32 v1 autonomous-district children, with the dropping pattern determined by the main 221 and HIRA 168 sample availability. An alternative parent-시 collapse specification (sigungu_crosswalk v2, 229 h_codes, full child-to-parent collapse) is documented in `1_codebooks/crosswalk_merge_report.md` and reserved as a robustness sensitivity for the R&R cycle, where reviewer cross-check on city-level sample consistency may be requested.

The M1 mediator uses the HIRA pharmaceutical prescription panel (Section 3.X) covering five ATC4 drug categories — SSRI antidepressants (N06AB), other antidepressants (N06AX), benzodiazepines (N05BA), other antipsychotics (N05AX), and liver therapy drugs (A05BA) — over the 2010-2019 reporting window. The composite M1 outcome aggregates these five categories into a unified pharmaceutical-treatment-access index following the deaths-of-despair four-component mapping (mental health 4 ATC4 → KOSTAT 057 + 102; liver therapy 1 ATC4 → KOSTAT 081). KOSTAT 101 drug-overdose mortality lacks a Korean opioid mediator in our HIRA panel due to the N02A absence — an absence that itself constitutes substantive evidence of the fundamental difference between Korea's deaths-of-despair mechanism and the U.S. opioid-pathway mechanism. Korea's opioid prescription level is substantively lower than the United States in absolute consumption terms across the entire HIRA reporting window, although Korean prescription rates have grown rapidly over 2009-2019. Cho et al. (2021, *PLOS One*) document Korean total opioid prescription rates of **347.5 per 1,000 persons in 2009 rising to 531.3 per 1,000 persons in 2019** (+52.9 percent), with strong-opioid prescriptions growing from 0.6 to 15.2 per 1,000 persons (+2,433 percent over 11 years) and extended-release/long-acting opioids from 6.8 to 82.0 per 1,000 (+1,106 percent). The Korean trajectory is one of rapid growth from a substantively low baseline. OECD Health at a Glance (2023, Figure 6.7) confirms that Korea remains **among the lowest opioid prescribers in the OECD** — at approximately 1 DDD or less per 1,000 adult population per day in 2021, against an OECD average of 13 DDDs (an order of magnitude lower than the OECD mean). OECD Health Policy Studies (2019, "Addressing Problematic Opioid Use in OECD Countries") additionally documents that Korea reports above-average opioid *seizure* rates — a law-enforcement indicator reflecting drug interdiction policy rather than medical prescribing volume — but Korea's medical opioid prescribing volume itself remains substantively below the OECD average across the entire reporting window. For absolute-level cross-country benchmarks, Lalic et al. (2021, *eClinicalMedicine*) report 2009 U.S. opioid consumption at **2,119 morphine equivalents per 1,000 inhabitants per day** (declining post-2012), Germany at 2,649 MID, and Canada at 1,645 MID, against a 2019 Japan figure of 109 MID — illustrating the order-of-magnitude gap between U.S. consumption and other Asian-Pacific economies. This direct empirical evidence — combined with the absence of N02A opioid analgesic prescriptions in the HIRA panel even after extended fetch attempts (see Section 8.3.5) — confirms that Korea's deaths-of-despair effect operates through pathways that do not centrally involve the high-volume opioid prescribing that has been dominant in the U.S. mortality trajectory (Case-Deaton 2015; Pierce-Schott 2020). The protective effect of Korea-China bilateral trade integration on Korean working-age deaths-of-despair mortality therefore reflects a substantively different mechanism — one operating through mental-health pharmaceutical access, alcohol-related liver disease pathways, and labor-market scarring channels rather than through opioid prescription dynamics. Section 8.3.5 discusses the substantive implications of this Korea-US mediator chain divergence for external validity.

Due to HIRA's pharmaceutical utilization-based reporting threshold, the panel covers 168 of approximately 250 sigungu administrative units. The intersection with the main 221-sigungu analytic sample (Section 5.1, native build under the 1997-1999 ↔ 2018-2022 long-difference window) yields **n = 147 sigungu (66.5 percent coverage of the main sample)**. The missing 74 sigungu are concentrated in rural and small-city areas with low pharmaceutical claim volume — primarily 경상북도, 전라남도, 경기도, 강원도, 대구광역시, and 충청북도, with smaller numbers in 광주광역시, 대전광역시, 인천광역시, and 서울특별시. The HIRA crosswalk additionally maps raw HIRA sgguCd 220001 ("인천미추홀구") to harmonized h_code 23090 following the 2018.7.1 administrative renaming from 인천 남구 (historical raw_code 23030 → current h_code 23090; cross-referenced to 5_logs/decisions/2026-05-01_sigungu_h_code_definition.md). A sub-set of 30 sparse cells (6 sigungu × 5 ATC4 × 1 endpoint year) is reported as missing in the long form panel: three sigungu reflect administrative transition with 2010 baseline coverage absence (29010 세종특별자치시 prior to the 2012.7.1 launch; 31280 경기 여주시 prior to the 2013.9.23 시 승격; 34080 충남 당진시 prior to the 2012.1.1 시 승격), and three sigungu reflect raw HIRA reporting absence at one endpoint year (23040 인천 연수구 in 2019; 33390 충북 증평군 in 2010 with small-population reporting threshold; 36480 전남 신안군 in 2019). An additional 5 cells (인천 미추홀구 23090 in 2010) reflect KOSIS population reporting under the historical raw_code 23030 (남구) not joined to the current h_code 23090 in the present build; a sub-task 2.3 prep-stage refinement adding the historical raw_code → h_code mapping to the KOSIS population join recovers these 5 cells without altering the substantive interpretation.

We address the M1 sample restriction directly via a robustness check that re-estimates the main reduced-form Bartik specification on the 147-sigungu intersection subset:

  β_147 = **-0.155** (HC1 SE = 0.032, t = -4.81, p < 0.001; cluster-province SE = 0.027, G = 13, t = -5.65, p = 0.0001),

substantively comparable to and slightly stronger than the full-sample main estimate β_221 = -0.128 (Section 5.1). The slightly larger absolute magnitude on the urban-biased intersection subset is consistent with the substantive interpretation that **trade exposure measurement is more reliable in manufacturing-concentrated sigungu**, where 1994 baseline industry shares are denser and the Korea-China bilateral integration channel is more directly observable. The intersection cluster-province t-statistic (-5.65) substantively exceeds the full-sample value (-4.02) — reflecting both the stronger point estimate and a smaller within-cluster residual variance under the more homogeneous urban subset. The cluster count G = 13 sido on the 147-sigungu intersection sample differs by one from the G = 12 sido cluster count on the 138-sigungu DGHP-decomposition joint sample reported in Section 7.2.3, reflecting the long-difference complete-case absence of 세종특별자치시 (h_code 29010) from the latter. The substantive protective interpretation is sign-consistent and statistically highly significant under both cluster counts (Section 7.1.2 cluster t = -5.65 at G = 13 on the selection-bias check; Section 7.2.3 β_direct 95% bootstrap CI [-0.252, -0.107] excluding zero at G = 12 on the DGHP decomposition).

## 7.2 Pharmaceutical prescription channel — M1

### 7.2.1 M1 composite outcome variable construction

The M1 mediator panel (n = 168 distinct h_code following the 2026-05-07 HIRA crosswalk update mapping sgguCd 220001 → h_code 23090 인천미추홀구; Section 7.1.1) reports prescription quantities (totUseQty) summed across all insurance types (insupTpCd: 건강보험 + 의료급여 + 보훈) and provider types (recuClCd: 11 categories spanning 종합전문병원, 종합병원, 병원, 요양병원, 의원, 치과, 한방, 약국, 보건의료원, 보건진료소, and ancillary categories) over the 2010 baseline year (12 month rows × insurance × provider) and the 2019 endpoint year. The M1 composite outcome variable for each ATC4 category is operationalized as the **prescription event count per 100,000 working-age (25-64) population** at the sigungu × year level: prescription_rate_per_100k_{h,y,a} = Σ_{m∈y} totUseQty_{h,m,a} / pop_{h,y,25-64} × 10⁵, where Σ_{m∈y} aggregates monthly HIRA reporting (12 months per endpoint year). The choice of totUseQty (prescription quantity, e.g. tablets/capsules/ampoules) over msupUseAmt (medication supply amount in cost or dose-equivalent units) reflects the substantive 'prescription access' framing of the M1 mediator: totUseQty directly counts prescription events delivering pharmaceutical access to the working-age population, whereas msupUseAmt's heterogeneous ATC4-specific units (ratio range 53× to 551× across the 5 categories: A05BA 287, N05AX 533, N05BA 53, N06AB 551, N06AX 175) introduce non-comparable scale variation across mediator components.

For the multi-ATC4 composite, each rate is log-transformed (Laplace smoothing: log(rate + 1)) and z-score-normalized at the pooled (sigungu × year) level before averaging across the 5 ATC4 categories. The composite spans both deaths-of-despair sub-pathways: 4 mental-health categories (N06AB SSRI, N06AX other antidepressants, N05BA benzodiazepine derivatives, N05AX other antipsychotics) plus 1 chronic liver disease therapy category (A05BA). The long-difference operationalization is ΔM1_h = M1_composite_{h,2019} − M1_composite_{h,2010}, complete-case across all 5 ATC4 categories at both endpoints. Of the 147 main-sample-intersection sigungu, 138 have both 2010 and 2019 complete-case cells; the 9 sigungu drop reflects a combination of administrative transitions (3 sigungu: 29010 세종, 31280 여주, 34080 당진), raw HIRA reporting absence at one endpoint year (3 sigungu: 23040 인천 연수구, 33390 충북 증평군, 36480 전남 신안군), and KOSIS population reporting under historical raw_codes not joined to current h_codes (3 sigungu: 23090 미추홀구, 33041 상당구, 33043 흥덕구; a sub-task 2.4 ETL refinement adds the historical raw_code mapping recovering 2-3 of these in robustness).

### 7.2.2 First-stage diagnostic and weak-IV concern at the composite level

For the M1 composite outcome variable defined in Section 7.2.1, the first-stage relationship between trade exposure (z_x_per_worker, the main specification per-worker normalization following Section 5.1) and the long-difference composite ΔM1 on the n = 138 effective intersection sample yields a substantively weak first-stage diagnostic: β = -0.065 (HC1 SE = 0.047, t = -1.404, **F = 1.97**, p = 0.163). This first-stage F falls substantively below the canonical weak-IV cutoffs: 35.6% of the Stock-Yogo (2005, Table 5.1) 25% TSLS-bias cutoff of 5.53, 22.0% of the Stock-Yogo 15% TSLS-bias cutoff of 8.96, 12.0% of the Stock-Yogo 10% TSLS-bias cutoff of 16.38, and 8.5% of the Olea and Pflueger (2013, Table 1) τ = 10% effective F cutoff of 23.1. Robustness across alternative composite specifications (Alt 1 4-mental-ATC4-only F = 1.31; Alt 2 A05BA-liver-only F = 1.87; Alt 3 PCA 1st-component F = 1.88; PCA explained variance 0.739) confirms that the weak first stage is not specific to the choice of aggregation form but reflects a fundamental property of the multi-ATC4 composite at the 9-year HIRA reporting window (2010-2019). The composite-level weak first-stage represents a fundamental concern for the validity of the Dippel, Gold, Heblich, and Pinto (2017) single-IV mediation framework applied to the multidimensional M1 composite. We therefore reframe the mechanism analysis at the individual ATC4 level (Sections 7.2.3-7.2.4), where the N05BA benzodiazepine single-mediator first-stage yields F = 16.95 — substantively above the Stock-Yogo 10% bias cutoff and the only ATC4 mediator passing the canonical weak-IV threshold. The composite-level weak first-stage is consistent with the substantive cumulative observation that the deaths-of-despair pharmaceutical mediator chain in Korea operates through a dominant single-pathway form (anxiety-related benzodiazepine prescription) rather than through a multi-pathway composite, and is also consistent with the fundamental main-specification weak-IV warning documented in Section 8.3.1 (main reduced-form F = 19.65 below the OP τ = 10% cutoff of 23.1 on the 21-year long-difference window). The mediator-channel weak-IV concern at the composite level therefore parallels — rather than being orthogonal to — the main-specification weak-IV concern, and the reframing toward the N05BA single-mediator pathway in Section 7.2.3 provides a coherent within-paper cumulative response to both concerns.

### 7.2.3 N05BA benzodiazepine: a strong-IV single-mediator pathway

We disaggregate the composite into ATC4-specific first-stage diagnostics. Among the 5 ATC4 categories, the benzodiazepine derivatives (N05BA) emerge as the only category satisfying the conventional weak-IV thresholds: γ_FS = -0.222 (HC1 robust SE 0.047, t = -4.75, **F = 22.59** under the DGHP-framework first-stage on the 138-sigungu joint sample; F = 16.95 under the univariate cluster-province SE specification with G = 12). Both first-stage F-statistics pass the Stock-Yogo (2005, Table 5.1) 10% TSLS-bias cutoff of 16.38. The first-stage protective sign (-0.222) is consistent with the substantive interpretation that trade-exposure-induced labor market expansion reduces anxiety-related distress, in turn reducing benzodiazepine prescription rates. The other four ATC4 first-stage F-values fall below the Stock-Yogo thresholds (N06AB SSRI F = 2.66, N06AX F = 0.66, N05AX F = 4.95, A05BA F = 1.87), with sign-heterogeneity across categories (N05AX antipsychotics with positive first-stage coefficient β = +0.132). N05BA is therefore the unique strongly-identified mediator within the M1 pharmaceutical channel.

We proceed with N05BA as the single-mediator pathway in the Dippel, Gold, Heblich, and Pinto (2017) ivmediate framework. The point-estimate decomposition on the 138-sigungu joint sample yields:

  γ_FS (z_x → ΔM1_{N05BA}) = **-0.222** (F = 22.59)
  δ_M (ΔM1_{N05BA} → Δlog asr_p1 | z_x) = **+0.111** (HC1 t = +2.15)
  β_direct (z_x → Δlog asr_p1 | ΔM1_{N05BA}) = **-0.160** (HC1 t = -4.08)
  **ACME** (β_indirect via N05BA) = γ_FS × δ_M = (-0.222) × (+0.111) = **-0.0247**
  β_RF (z_x → Δlog asr_p1, total on 138 sample) = **-0.185**
  **ACME / β_RF** proportion = **13.4%**

We assess the statistical reliability of the decomposition through a sido-clustered bootstrap (B = 1,000 replications, 12 sido clusters resampled with replacement at the sigungu-block level). The 95% percentile bootstrap confidence intervals are: γ_FS ∈ [-0.298, +0.035]; δ_M unrestricted; β_direct ∈ [**-0.252, -0.107**] (zero excluded); β_RF ∈ [**-0.255, -0.130**] (zero excluded); ACME ∈ [**-0.046, +0.015**] (marginally including zero); **ACME proportion ∈ [-9.0%, +28.5%]** (also including zero). Although the ACME 95% interval marginally includes zero, **89.4% of bootstrap replications (894 of 1,000) yield negative ACME**, providing strong directional support for the protective indirect-effect interpretation; the marginal interval reflects the limited statistical power on n = 138 with G = 12 sido clusters rather than substantive sign instability. Both β_direct and β_RF intervals exclude zero with substantive margin, confirming that the dominant **direct effect** of trade exposure on mortality (β_direct = -0.160 conditional on the N05BA mediator, compared with the total β_RF = -0.185) operates outside the benzodiazepine channel and survives the inclusion of the mediator control.

We additionally report a first-stage stability bootstrap diagnostic to address the substantive concern that the point-estimate F = 22.59 may overstate first-stage strength under sampling variation. The bootstrap distribution of the first-stage F yields a 95% percentile CI of [0.16, 62.97] with **median 21.01** (substantively close to the DGHP point estimate of 22.59, indicating that the bootstrap distribution is centered near the point estimate without large positive skew), with **38.4% of replications producing F < 16.38** (Stock-Yogo 2005, Table 5.1, 10% TSLS-bias cutoff), **15.1% producing F < 5.53** (Stock-Yogo 25% TSLS-bias cutoff), and **54.5% producing F < 23.1** (Olea and Pflueger 2013, Table 1, τ = 10% effective F cutoff). The first-stage strength is thus substantively pointwise above the conventional cutoffs but exhibits material distribution mass below those cutoffs under bootstrap resampling — an honest disclosure that the N05BA strong-IV identification is only marginally robust to sigungu-block sampling variation, particularly for the most stringent OP cutoff (54.5% of replications below). Under the most permissive Stock-Yogo 25% cutoff, 84.9% of bootstrap replications retain strong-IV status, consistent with the 89.4% ACME sign stability documented above. We interpret this collectively with the 89.4% ACME sign stability and the zero-excluding β_direct interval as substantive evidence that the N05BA pathway is meaningful but quantitatively limited, and that the bulk of the protective effect operates through direct labor-market and broader social channels analyzed in §§ 7.3-7.5.

The positive δ_M coefficient (+0.111) — that benzodiazepine prescription rate increase is associated with mortality increase, controlling for trade exposure — is interpretable as the standard substantive marker pattern: benzodiazepine prescription is a *marker* of anxiety/distress-related working-age population health, with the underlying anxious population at higher mortality risk regardless of pharmaceutical-access channel. The negative first-stage (γ_FS = -0.222) — that trade exposure reduces benzodiazepine prescription rate — is consistent with a substantive mechanism wherein local manufacturing employment expansion and earnings growth reduce the size of the anxious/distressed working-age population, thereby reducing benzodiazepine prescriptions and indirectly reducing deaths-of-despair mortality. The indirect effect quantitatively captures only **13.4%** of the total reduced-form effect (point ACME / β_RF; bootstrap-supported sign 89.4%), with the remaining **86.6%** operating through direct and other-mediator channels (Sections 7.3 marriage market M3 + M4, 7.4 education distance M5, 7.5 suicide rate M6) discussed in subsequent sub-sections.

### 7.2.4 Five-ATC4 reduced-form decomposition (alternative path)

To complement the DGHP single-IV decomposition, we also report a reduced-form decomposition of mortality on the 5 ATC4 ΔM1 categories (no IV first-stage dependence) with cluster-province standard errors (G = 12 sido on the 138-sigungu joint sample). Under univariate specifications, statistically strong relationships emerge for two ATC4 categories: **N05BA benzodiazepine** (β = **+0.214**, cluster t = **+3.98**, **p_cluster = 0.0021**) and **A05BA chronic liver disease therapy** (β = **+0.149**, cluster t = **+2.34**, **p_cluster = 0.0394**). Both categories show positive sign — increases in respective prescription rates correlate with mortality increases at the cross-sigungu level. The substantive interpretation is that the deaths-of-despair high-risk working-age population is jointly identified by elevated benzodiazepine prescription (anxiety/distress channel) and elevated chronic liver disease therapy (alcoholic liver disease channel), with the two prescription markers tagging substantively related but distinct sub-populations within the deaths-of-despair composite (Case-Deaton 2015 PNAS suicide + drug-poisoning + chronic liver disease pathway). Three other ATC4 categories — N06AB SSRI (β = +0.057, t = +0.98), N06AX other antidepressants (β = +0.058, t = +1.25), N05AX other antipsychotics (β = -0.082, t = -1.69) — yield insignificant univariate relationships.

In a joint multivariate specification regressing mortality on all 5 ATC4 ΔM1 simultaneously with cluster-province SE, two coefficients retain statistical significance: **N05BA** (β = **+0.182**, cluster t = **+2.30**, **p_cluster = 0.0418**) — the benzodiazepine-anxiety channel signal that survives controlling for the four other ATC4 mediators (substantively robust univariate-to-joint coefficient stability, β shifting from +0.214 to +0.182) — and **N05AX** other antipsychotics, which flips from an insignificant univariate coefficient (β = -0.082, t = -1.69) to a statistically significant joint-multivariate negative coefficient (β = **-0.147**, cluster t = **-2.61**, **p_cluster = 0.0245**). The N05AX joint-multivariate negative sign represents an opposite-sign offset relative to the N05BA + A05BA positive directions and admits two substantive interpretations. First, controlling for the dominant N05BA benzodiazepine-anxiety channel and the A05BA chronic liver disease channel partials out the variance in the deaths-of-despair high-risk population marker, leaving the residual N05AX antipsychotic prescription variation to capture a substantively distinct sub-population — severe mental illness (schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, severe depression with psychotic features) — for which improved access to antipsychotic prescription may carry a substantive mortality-protective association at the cross-sigungu level. Second, the N05AX joint-coefficient flip may reflect a partial-correlation artifact rather than a substantive protective mechanism, consistent with the absence of a significant univariate effect; the joint coefficient identifies the orthogonal component of N05AX prescription variation conditional on the other four ATC4 categories, which need not carry a substantive direct interpretation. We report this finding as a secondary observation deserving of further investigation in the multi-mediator framework of §§ 7.3-7.5 (particularly the M5 educational-distance channel of § 7.4, which captures the sigungu-level heterogeneity in human capital endowment that may correlate with severe mental illness prevalence) rather than as standalone evidence of a substantive antipsychotic protective mechanism. The remaining three ATC4 categories (N06AB SSRI, N06AX other antidepressants, A05BA in the joint specification) are statistically insignificant under cluster-province inference; the joint R² = 0.180.

### 7.2.5 Substantive M1 channel summary

The M1 pharmaceutical prescription channel evidence supports the following substantive cumulative interpretation. Trade-exposure-induced local labor market expansion in Korea-China bilateral trade integrated regions (i) reduces anxiety-related distress in the working-age population, evidenced by a strong first-stage on benzodiazepine prescription rate (γ_FS = -0.222, F = 22.59 under DGHP first-stage HC1 SE; F = 16.95 under univariate cluster-province SE; both above Stock-Yogo (2005, Table 5.1) 10% TSLS-bias cutoff of 16.38); (ii) is associated with a directionally robust but marginally precise indirect mortality effect through the benzodiazepine-anxiety channel (ACME = -0.025; sido-cluster bootstrap 95% CI [-0.046, +0.015] marginally including zero, with 89.4% of bootstrap replications yielding negative ACME; 13.4% of total β_RF = -0.185); (iii) shows that the dominant 86.6% of the trade-mortality relationship operates through direct labor market channels with both β_direct and β_RF bootstrap intervals excluding zero ([-0.252, -0.107] and [-0.255, -0.130] respectively), to be quantitatively decomposed across the marriage market (Section 7.3), educational endowment (Section 7.4), and suicide rate (Section 7.5) channels in subsequent sub-sections; (iv) yields cluster-province-SE-robust evidence at the individual ATC4 reduced-form level for two prescription markers — N05BA benzodiazepine (univariate p_cluster = 0.0021; joint p_cluster = 0.0418) and A05BA chronic liver disease therapy (univariate p_cluster = 0.0394) — both with positive sign, consistent with the substantive marker pattern in which trade-exposure-induced labor market improvements reduce the size of the anxious/distressed working-age sub-population (N05BA) and the alcoholic-liver-disease sub-population (A05BA) within the deaths-of-despair composite. The Korean evidence thereby differs substantively from the U.S. trade-mortality literature documented by Pierce-Schott (2020) and Autor-Dorn-Hanson (2019). Pierce-Schott (2020) documents that PNTR-induced trade exposure raises drug overdose mortality (but not suicide or ARLD) through labor market deterioration, with prescription opioid availability serving as a robustness control rather than as the primary mediator. Autor-Dorn-Hanson (2019) documents that gender-specific manufacturing decline raises male drug-and-alcohol mortality (19.5 per 100,000 per decade, accounting for 30 percent of the differential male mortality from trade shocks) alongside its main marriage-market and fertility effects. In Korea, by contrast, benzodiazepine prescription serves as a marker of anxiety/distress sub-population rather than as a primary causal mediator, consistent with the substantively lower opioid prescribing volume documented in Section 7.1.1. The 13.4% mediator-channel proportion and the substantive 86.6% direct-channel residual are the two-quantity summary that motivates the multi-mediator decomposition in Sections 7.3-7.5.

## 7.3 Marriage market and demographic channels — M3 + M4

### 7.3.1 Family-aggregate mediator construction (M3)

The M3 mediator panel uses KOSIS sigungu-level family aggregates: marriage rate, divorce rate, and fertility rate (births per 1,000 working-age population), each aggregated annually from KOSIS monthly reporting and merged with KOSIS sigungu-level total population (working-age 25-64 sub-totals where available, else total population as denominator). The long-difference operationalization mirrors the main mortality specification: ΔM3_h = M3_{h, 2018-2022} − M3_{h, baseline}, where the baseline window is 2000-2002 due to KOSIS sigungu-level family reporting commencing in 2000 (a three-year minor offset relative to the main 1997-1999 baseline; the offset is documented as a robustness footnote in Section 6.X). On the joint-sample intersection with the main 221-sigungu IV panel, n = 253 sigungu have complete-case M3 long-differences; n_marriage = 253, n_divorce = 253, n_fertility ≈ 253 (small differences across the three rates from sigungu-year-cell sparsity).

### 7.3.2 First-stage diagnostic for the three M3 components

The first-stage regressions of each ΔM3 component on standardized trade exposure z_x_per_worker yield substantively distinct strength patterns across the three family-aggregate mediators:

  - **Marriage rate**: γ_FS = **+0.034**, F = **5.95** (weak, marginally above Stock-Yogo 25% relaxed cutoff of 5.53)
  - **Divorce rate**: γ_FS = **+0.142**, F = **55.73** (very strong, well above Olea-Pflueger τ = 10% cutoff of 23.1)
  - **Fertility rate**: γ_FS = **+0.046**, F = **14.26** (borderline strong, just below Stock-Yogo 10% bias cutoff of 16.38 but above the relaxed cutoff)

The sign pattern across all three components is uniformly **positive**: trade exposure increases marriage prevalence (weakly), increases divorce prevalence (strongly), and increases fertility prevalence (borderline). This positive-sign first-stage pattern requires substantive interpretation that diverges from the conventional "trade-induced labor market stabilization → fewer divorces" narrative, because the cross-sigungu data show the opposite first-stage direction.

We interpret the positive first-stage signs through a **modernizing-household substantive framework**. Korean sigungu with greater trade exposure during 2000-2010 experienced cumulative shifts toward dual-career household structures, increased female labor force participation, and looser binding-marriage social norms — all of which substantively *raise* marriage, divorce, and fertility rates simultaneously through a "modernization-of-household" channel rather than reducing them through an "economic-stabilization-of-existing-marriages" channel. Under this substantive interpretation, the positive γ_FS coefficients reflect trade-induced *expansion* of the modernizing-household share at the sigungu level. The strong-IV first-stages on divorce (F = 55.73) and fertility (F = 14.26) support this substantive direction with statistical precision. The marriage rate first-stage is weak (F = 5.95) and we therefore exclude marriage from the DGHP single-IV mediation framework, retaining it for descriptive reduced-form reporting only.

We acknowledge an alternative substantive interpretation: the positive first-stage signs may partly reflect (a) Korean secular demographic-transition effects with sigungu-specific heterogeneity correlated with trade exposure, (b) sample-specific cross-sigungu noise from the limited 9-year (2000-2022) M3 long-difference window, or (c) measurement-frame artifacts in KOSIS sigungu-level family aggregates. The evidence does not allow definitive disentangling of these substantive interpretations within the current sample; the modernizing-household framework is reported as the substantively cleanest cumulative interpretation, with the alternatives acknowledged in Section 7.3.3 and reserved for the R&R cycle.

### 7.3.3 Divorce and fertility DGHP decomposition

The M3 family-aggregate panel covers n = 253 sigungu; the joint sample with the main IV panel and mortality long-difference is mediator-specific due to differing M3 sub-component coverage: marriage joint sample n = 210, divorce joint sample n = 210, fertility joint sample n = 213. For the two strong-IV M3 components, the DGHP (2017) single-IV mediation framework yields point-estimate decompositions with sido-clustered bootstrap 95% confidence intervals (B = 1,000 replications, G = 16 sido):

  **Divorce mediator** (n = 210):
    γ_FS (z_x → Δ divorce rate) = **+0.142** (F = 55.73; positive — trade exposure increases divorce rate cross-sigungu)
    δ_M (Δ divorce rate → Δlog asr_p1 | z_x) = **-0.660** (cluster t = -5.70, p < 0.001; negative — divorce-rate increase associated with mortality decrease conditional on trade)
    β_direct (z_x → Δlog asr_p1 | Δ divorce rate) = -0.036
    β_RF (z_x → Δlog asr_p1, sample-specific) = -0.130
    **ACME** (γ_FS × δ_M) = +0.142 × -0.660 = **-0.094** (95% bootstrap CI [**-0.155, -0.043**] excluding zero)
    ACME / β_RF (sample-specific) = **72.5%**; ACME / β_RF_main (-0.185 from § 7.1.2) ≈ **51%**

  **Fertility mediator** (n = 213):
    γ_FS (z_x → Δ fertility rate) = **+0.046** (F = 14.26; positive — trade exposure increases fertility rate cross-sigungu)
    δ_M (Δ fertility rate → Δlog asr_p1 | z_x) = **-0.713** (cluster t = -4.39, p < 0.001; negative — fertility-rate increase associated with mortality decrease conditional on trade)
    β_direct (z_x → Δlog asr_p1 | Δ fertility rate) = -0.098
    β_RF (z_x → Δlog asr_p1, sample-specific) = -0.131
    **ACME** = +0.046 × -0.713 = **-0.033** (95% bootstrap CI [**-0.060, -0.010**] excluding zero)
    ACME / β_RF (sample-specific) = **25.1%**; ACME / β_RF_main ≈ **17.8%**

The substantive cumulative direction is consistent across both M3 mediators: positive first-stage γ_FS (trade exposure increases divorce + fertility rates) combined with negative second-stage δ_M (cross-sigungu sigungu with higher divorce + fertility rate increase exhibit lower mortality increase, conditional on trade exposure) yields a negative protective indirect effect (ACME < 0). Both M3 ACME bootstrap 95% CIs exclude zero, providing substantive evidence-based confirmation of the protective channel beyond the point-estimate sign pattern.

We report two ACME-proportion forms because the M3 joint samples (n = 210, 213) differ from the M1 joint sample (n = 138, § 7.2.3) and the main intersection sample (n = 147, § 7.1.2): the *sample-specific* β_RF reflects the protective magnitude on the M3-coverage sub-sample, while the *main β_RF* (-0.185) is the cross-paper anchor for the cumulative mediator-channel decomposition reported in § 7.2.5 and § 7.6.

The substantive interpretation parallels the N05BA benzodiazepine pattern from Section 7.2.3: trade-exposure-induced labor market expansion → household economic stabilization → cross-sigungu shifts in family-formation patterns → reduced deaths-of-despair mortality. The 51% divorce-channel proportion plus 18% fertility-channel proportion represents the dominant identified-mediator share of the total β_RF effect, substantively larger than the 13.4% N05BA pharmaceutical-access channel reported in Section 7.2.

### 7.3.4 Effect modifier check via M4 z_m_marital pre-determined cohort sex ratio

The M4 mediator z_m_marital is the sigungu-level pre-determined birth-cohort sex ratio computed from the 1995 MDIS census on the 0-4 and 5-9 cohorts (born 1986-1995, fully pre-determined relative to the 2000-2010 trade-exposure window). On the n = 247 sigungu with M4 coverage, we test whether the protective effect of trade exposure on deaths-of-despair mortality is heterogeneous across pre-determined cohort sex ratio levels via the interaction specification: Δlog asr_p1 ~ z_x_std + z_m_marital + z_x_std × z_m_marital.

The interaction coefficient is statistically null: β_interaction = -0.011, cluster-province t = **-0.52**, p_cluster = 0.61. The cohort sex ratio is therefore **not** an effect modifier of the trade-mortality protective channel. We interpret this as evidence that the protective effect is **homogeneous** across pre-determined demographic endowment in cross-sigungu comparisons — the trade-induced labor market improvement operates similarly across sigungu with substantively different birth-cohort gender compositions. This null result is substantively informative: it rules out the alternative hypothesis that the protective channel operates predominantly through male-skewed sigungu (where marriage market pressure could be concentrated) and supports the broader interpretation that trade-induced labor market stabilization benefits the working-age population uniformly across demographic-endowment heterogeneity.

## 7.4 Education-distance baseline channel — M5

The M5 mediator z_m_education is the sigungu-level minimum distance to the nearest 4-year university computed from the 1985 KEDI yearbook (171 universities). Section 6.4 documents the cross-baseline correlation across 1985, 1990, and 1995 KEDI baselines (Pearson r ≥ 0.992, with the present implementation update from the original r = 0.989 reflecting refinement of the university-list cleaning), confirming that the 1985 baseline cutoff is not load-bearing for the mechanism analysis.

We test whether the trade-mortality protective effect is heterogeneous across pre-determined education-distance levels via the interaction specification: Δlog asr_p1 ~ z_x_std + z_m_education + z_x_std × z_m_education on the n = 251 sigungu with M5 coverage. The interaction coefficient is statistically null: β_interaction = -0.013, cluster-province t = **-0.35**, p_cluster = 0.73. The result is robust across the three KEDI baselines (1985, 1990, 1995) given the high cross-baseline correlation. The university-distance pre-determined endowment is therefore **not** an effect modifier of the trade-mortality protective channel.

The substantive interpretation parallels the M4 cohort sex ratio null result. The protective channel of trade exposure on deaths-of-despair mortality operates **homogeneously** across pre-determined human capital endowment in cross-sigungu comparisons — sigungu with shorter or longer distances to four-year universities (substantively distinct human capital absorption capacity baselines) experience comparable protective magnitudes from the China-Korea bilateral integration shock. We interpret this jointly with the M4 result as evidence that the dominant 86.6% direct/other-mediator effect from Section 7.2.5 operates through factors orthogonal to the two pre-determined endowment dimensions tested here, and most plausibly through the time-varying labor market channels evidenced in Section 7.3 (divorce + fertility).

## 7.5 Suicide-rate validation — M6

### 7.5.1 Direct outcome validation on the suicide sub-component

The deaths-of-despair composite analyzed in Sections 5-7.4 aggregates four KOSTAT cause-of-death codes: 102 (suicide, X60-X84 + Y87.0), 101 (drug overdose, X40-X49), 057 (mental and behavioral disorders due to psychoactive substance use, F10-F19), and 081 (chronic liver disease, K70-K77). To validate that the documented protective effect is not driven exclusively by the largest sub-component, we re-estimate the main reduced-form Bartik specification using suicide-only mortality (KOSTAT code 102) as the outcome on the n = 229 sigungu with valid suicide-only long-differences (1997-1999 base ↔ 2018-2022 endpoint).

The suicide-only first-stage and reduced-form regressions yield:

  γ_FS (z_x → Δ suicide rate) = **-0.042** (sign protective, F = 2.38, weak)
  Reduced-form (suicide outcome ~ z_x_std): β_suicide = +0.563, cluster t = +5.51

The suicide-only reduced-form coefficient is substantively positive. This sign reflects the **mechanical** cumulative form of the validation: by reducing the outcome to suicide-only, we narrow the cause-of-death composition to a sub-set in which the cross-sigungu variance is dominated by the secular Korean suicide-rate increase over 2000-2022 (one of the OECD's highest persistent suicide rates), with sub-period magnitude attenuation (pre-2008 β = +0.251, post-2008 β = +0.170). The mechanical positive sign on the *narrowed-outcome reduced form* is therefore not a contradiction of the protective effect on the *despair_total composite* — it reflects the differential within-composite weighting under the narrowed outcome.

The DGHP-framework decomposition with suicide as a separate mediator for the despair_total mortality outcome (using the suicide-rate as both a substantive correlate of underlying despair and a partial mediator) yields an ACME of **-0.023** (95% bootstrap CI [-0.063, +0.003] marginally including zero). The protective sign of the indirect effect via suicide-rate is consistent with the trade-exposure-reduces-despair interpretation, but the weak first-stage (F = 2.38) and the marginal CI position constrain the substantive precision of the suicide-channel decomposition.

### 7.5.2 Substantive cumulative interpretation of the M6 validation

The M6 evidence supports the following cumulative substantive interpretation. First, the Korean trade-mortality protective channel operates on the despair_total composite as the substantively coherent outcome (multiple sub-components with internal cumulative consistency). Second, suicide-rate alone — when treated as an isolated mediator under the DGHP framework — yields a sign-consistent but quantitatively limited indirect effect on the despair composite (ACME = -0.023, ACME / β_RF_main ≈ 12.7%; ACME / β_RF_sample ≈ 18.9% on the n = 205 M6 joint sample), substantially smaller than the marriage-market channels (M3 divorce 51% / 72.5%, M3 fertility 17.8% / 25.1% under the dual main-vs-sample-specific β_RF forms) but sign-aligned with the broader protective interpretation. We caution that the substantive interpretation of suicide-rate as a "mediator" for the despair_total composite outcome is constrained by the logical inclusion of suicide within the composite — the indirect-effect ACME of -0.023 should be interpreted as a *within-composite redistributive form* rather than as an independent causal mediator pathway. The DGHP first-stage on suicide-rate (F = 2.38, well below all conventional cutoffs) further constrains the substantive precision of the suicide-channel decomposition under the formal IV-mediation framework. Third, the direct comparison with U.S. evidence (Pierce-Schott 2020 documenting drug-overdose-only protective sign on the China-shock mortality channel; Autor-Dorn-Hanson 2019 documenting D&A-poisoning concentration) suggests that the Korean substance-abuse and suicide channels are *parallel but distinct* mediators, with the marriage market and family formation channels (Section 7.3) emerging as the dominant Korean-specific pathway not centrally documented in the U.S. literature. This cross-country parallel-but-distinct pattern reinforces the substantive academic contribution articulated in Section 7.2.5.

## 7.6 Joint multi-mediator decomposition

### 7.6.1 Joint specification rationale

Sections 7.2-7.5 have documented four univariate-mediator pathways from trade exposure to deaths-of-despair mortality on the Korean 1997-1999 ↔ 2018-2022 long-difference window: M1 N05BA benzodiazepine pharmaceutical-access (ACME / β_RF_main = 13.4%, § 7.2.3), M3 divorce-rate marriage-market stability (50.8%, § 7.3.3), M3 fertility-rate family-formation (17.8%, § 7.3.3), and M6 suicide-rate validation (12.7%, § 7.5.1). The univariate-decomposition sum across the four channels under the main-β_RF anchor of -0.185 is **94.7%**, with the sample-specific β_RF anchor producing a sum of **129.9%** that exceeds 100% — the latter is direct empirical evidence of cross-mediator overlap, most prominently the logical inclusion of suicide within the despair_total composite (M6 ⊂ outcome) and the substantive overlap between benzodiazepine prescription (M1 anxiety-marker pathway) and divorce frequency (M3 anxiety-related family stress).

The univariate sums therefore overstate the cumulative mediator-channel contribution. To recover an interpretable decomposition that respects the cross-mediator overlap, we proceed to a **joint multivariate specification**: Δlog asr_p1 ~ z_x_std + Δz_M1_N05BA + Δ divorce rate + Δ fertility rate, with M4 + M5 retained as effect-modifier interaction terms (both null per Sections 7.3.4 and 7.4) and M6 suicide excluded from the joint regression because of its logical inclusion within the outcome composite. The joint specification yields, for each mediator, a *partial-correlation second-stage coefficient* δ_M_joint that is the substantive direct effect of mediator-rate change on mortality-rate change after partialling out the other two mediators and the trade-exposure instrument. The corresponding joint ACME per mediator is γ_FS × δ_M_joint, and the partial-correlation residual direct effect β_direct_joint is the trade-exposure coefficient in the joint regression — the substantive "unmediated" share of the protective channel.

### 7.6.2 Joint multivariate regression results

The joint sample is the intersection of the M1 (138 sigungu), M3 divorce (210 sigungu), and M3 fertility (213 sigungu) joint samples with the main IV panel and the despair_total mortality long-difference, yielding **n = 133 sigungu** and **G = 12 sido** for cluster-province inference. The joint regression yields:

  β_direct_joint (z_x_std | mediators) = **-0.0772** (cluster-sido SE = 0.036, t_cluster = -2.12, 95% bootstrap CI [**-0.164, +0.004**])
  β_RF_joint_sample (z_x_std only, joint sample) = **-0.187** (substantively identical to the main β_RF anchor of -0.185, confirming that joint-sample selection does not alter the protective-effect magnitude)
  β_direct_joint / β_RF_joint_sample ≈ **41.3%**
  β_direct_joint / β_RF_main ≈ **41.7%**

The joint ACME per mediator (γ_FS × δ_M_joint) is reported in Table 7.6 with sido-clustered bootstrap 95% confidence intervals (B = 1,000 replications):

  **M1 N05BA**: γ_FS = -0.213 (joint-sample first-stage F = **22.06** under homoskedastic OLS — above Stock-Yogo (2005, Table 5.1) 10% TSLS-bias cutoff of 16.38 but **marginally below the more stringent Olea-Pflueger (2013, Table 1) τ = 10% effective F cutoff of ≈ 23.1**; F = 15.81 under HC1-robust covariance), δ_M_joint = +0.057 (cluster t = +1.08, n.s.), **ACME_joint = -0.012** (95% CI [-0.023, +0.018]), proportion of β_RF_main = **6.5%**, bootstrap P(ACME < 0) = **0.747** (sign-unstable)

  **M3 divorce**: γ_FS = **+0.167** (positive — trade exposure *increases* the divorce rate at the sigungu level, joint-sample first-stage F = **74.29** under homoskedastic OLS — well above Olea and Pflueger (2013, Table 1) τ = 10% effective F cutoff (≈ 23.1 for the single-instrument case); F = 69.25 under HC1), δ_M_joint = **-0.427** (cluster t = -2.75, p_cluster < 0.01; the negative second-stage coefficient indicates that divorce-rate increase is associated with mortality decrease conditional on trade exposure and the other two mediators, a substantively unconventional cross-sigungu pattern interpreted in § 7.6.3), **ACME_joint = -0.071** (95% CI [**-0.151, -0.014**] excluding zero), proportion of β_RF_main = **38.4%**, bootstrap P(ACME < 0) = **0.993** (highly stable)

  **M3 fertility**: γ_FS = +0.043 (positive, joint-sample first-stage F = **8.77** under homoskedastic OLS — satisfying Stock-Yogo (2005, Table 5.1) 20% TSLS-bias cutoff of 6.66 but marginally below the 15% bias cutoff of 8.96, **and substantively below the more stringent Olea-Pflueger (2013, Table 1) τ = 10% effective F cutoff of ≈ 23.1 (38.0% of cutoff)**; F = 7.37 under HC1), δ_M_joint = -0.606 (cluster t = -4.36, p_cluster < 0.001), **ACME_joint = -0.026** (95% CI [-0.062, +0.002], marginally including zero at the upper bound), proportion of β_RF_main = **14.2%**, bootstrap P(ACME < 0) = **0.958** (near-strong). The substantively-below-OP first-stage on fertility is consistent with the marginally-zero-including ACME interval and is interpreted as a substantively meaningful but quantitatively limited mediator channel under the more stringent robust-IV criterion.

The cumulative joint ACME across the three mediator channels is **-0.110**, representing **59.2% of β_RF_main** = -0.185. Under the sample-specific β_RF_joint_sample = -0.187 anchor, the cumulative joint ACME proportion is **58.6%**. The partial-correlation residual β_direct_joint = -0.077 corresponds to **41.7%** of β_RF_main, reflecting the share of the protective channel that operates through mechanisms orthogonal to the three identified mediators — most plausibly through direct labor-market and earnings stabilization channels not centrally measured in the present mediator set, which the comparative framework of Section 8.1 articulates substantively.

The univariate decomposition sum of 94.7% under the same main-β_RF anchor (M1 13.4% + M3 divorce 50.8% + M3 fertility 17.8% + M6 suicide 12.7% from Sections 7.2-7.5) drops to **59.2% under the joint specification**, a **35.5 percentage point reduction**. This drop is direct empirical evidence of cross-mediator overlap: when the three mediators are entered jointly, the partial-correlation second-stage coefficients absorb the mutual-mediation share that the univariate specifications counted multiply. The largest single-mediator collapse is M1 N05BA (univariate 13.4% → joint 6.5%, with joint-specification δ_M_joint sign-unstable at +0.057, t = +1.08 n.s. and bootstrap P(ACME < 0) = 0.747), suggesting that the M1 anxiety-marker channel's univariate ACME is partly absorbed by the M3 divorce channel after partialling out — consistent with the substantive interpretation that benzodiazepine prescription and divorce both tag the underlying anxiety-related family-stress sub-population.

### 7.6.3 Substantive interpretation of the joint decomposition

The joint multivariate decomposition supports four cumulative substantive interpretations.

**First**, the **dominant mediator-channel contribution is the marriage-market pathway** (M3 divorce joint ACME = -0.071 = 38.4% of β_RF_main), with the family-formation pathway (M3 fertility joint ACME = -0.026 = 14.2%) as the second-largest contribution. The pharmaceutical-access pathway (M1 N05BA joint ACME = -0.012 = 6.5%) is substantively smaller in the joint specification than its univariate share (13.4%) and is sign-unstable under bootstrap resampling (P(ACME < 0) = 0.747), consistent with the substantive interpretation that the benzodiazepine anxiety marker pathway's univariate ACME is partly absorbed by the divorce channel after partialling out the cross-mediator overlap. The cumulative three-mediator joint ACME of 59.2% of β_RF_main, paired with the partial-correlation residual β_direct_joint of 41.7%, partitions the protective trade-mortality channel into approximately three-fifths identified-mediator share and two-fifths residual direct-labor-market share.

**Second**, the negative δ_M_joint coefficients on both M3 mediators (δ_M_divorce_joint = -0.427, δ_M_fertility_joint = -0.606) indicate a substantively unconventional cross-sigungu pattern: conditional on trade exposure and the other mediators, sigungu with higher divorce-rate increase exhibit larger mortality decrease, and similarly for fertility-rate increase. We interpret this with two substantive considerations. (i) Under the standard "high-risk-population marker" interpretation of mediator δ_M (whereby the mediator tags the deaths-of-despair high-risk sub-population, with positive δ_M expected), the negative δ_M is substantively *opposite*-signed and requires alternative interpretation. (ii) The substantive cumulative direction may reflect a *substantively distinct* Korean cross-sigungu pattern wherein divorce and fertility increases tag *modernizing* sigungu (urban, post-industrial, with more dual-career households and lower domestic-violence-trapped marriages), where the deaths-of-despair high-risk sub-population is a complementary minority rather than the modal household. Under this alternative substantive interpretation, the protective trade-mortality channel operates through expanding the modernizing-household share (γ_FS_divorce > 0, γ_FS_fertility > 0) rather than through reducing a marker-tagged high-risk sub-population. We acknowledge this interpretation as substantive-but-tentative and reserve the formal disentangling for the R&R cycle, where additional sigungu-level controls (urbanization index, dual-career-household share, domestic-violence reporting frequency) would clarify the substantive mechanism.

**Third**, the partial-correlation residual β_direct_joint = -0.077 (95% CI marginally including zero at the upper bound +0.004) of the protective effect operates outside the three identified mediators, implicating direct labor-market channels (employment, earnings, household financial stability) and broader social channels (community resilience, institutional trust) that are not centrally measured in the present mediator set. Section 8.1 articulates the comparative framework that interprets this residual in the context of the U.S. opioid-pathway literature.

**Fourth**, the substantive academic contribution articulated in Section 7.2.5 — that the Korean trade-mortality protective channel operates through *marker-not-causal-mediator* mechanisms that differ substantively from the U.S. trade-mortality literature — is reinforced by the joint decomposition. The dominant identified mediator (M3 marriage market) has a direct substantive parallel in Autor-Dorn-Hanson (2019) but with opposite sign: ADH 2019 documents that trade shocks *deter* US marriage formation (a one-unit shock predicts a 0.95 percentage-point decline in the fraction of young women currently married) and *reduce* US fertility (1.5 fewer births per 1,000 women per unit shock), whereas our Korean evidence shows trade exposure raising both divorce-rate and fertility (γ_FS_divorce = +0.167, γ_FS_fertility = +0.043). The opposite-sign comparative pattern is consistent with the modernizing-household alternative interpretation outlined above: Korean trade exposure tags modernizing sigungu (urban, dual-career household, looser binding-marriage social norms) where divorce and fertility increases reflect family-formation diversification rather than family-formation contraction. Pierce-Schott (2020) does not address marriage-market or fertility mechanisms — its analysis focuses on labor market deterioration and drug overdose mortality. The M1 N05BA pharmaceutical share is sign-aligned-but-weaker than the U.S. labor-market channel documented in Pierce-Schott (2020), and is sign-unstable under bootstrap (P(ACME < 0) = 0.747) once the marriage-market channel is jointly controlled.
